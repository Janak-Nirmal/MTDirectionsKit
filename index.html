drop-in solution to show routes directly inside your iOS app

Did you ever wonder why Apple didn’t provide a way to show directions directly on top of MKMapView and force you to take your users to their built-in Maps.app just to guide them somewhere? Me too, that’s why I built MTDirectionsKit. MTDirectionsKit is an easy-to-use and easy to integrate framework for iOS that let's you do just that - guiding your users in the easiest and most professional way available.

MTDirectionsKit is...

Smart

MTDirectionsKit makes it as easy as possible for you to get the best result for your customers. It automatically detects the preferred system of measurement of the user and does all computions in kilometer or miles, depending on the preferrence. Of course you can overwrite the used system of measurement too, if you want to.

Versatile

MTDirectionsKit can be used with 2 different APIs: Google Directions API and MapQuest Open Directions API. A lot of parts of MTDirectionsKit are configurable and it even allows for hooks to customize the computed directions right before they get displayed to the user.

Easy to use

MTDirectionsKit handles various pitfalls that you encounter during development for you and helps you in every aspect possible. For example it can automatically set it's directionsDelegate when you set the delegate of MKMapView and reports why something went wrong, in case of an error.

Beautiful

The directions drawn on top of MKMapView are beautifully handcrafted and look stunning on Retina displays as well as regular ones. It's very easy to change the color of the overlay too.

Fast

MTDirectionsKit embraces the use of GCD for parsing of the data to not slow down your app and to keep it as snappy as always.

Reliable

MTDirectionsKit is Unit-Tested to ensure it's working as you and your users would expect.

Integration

There are basically 2 ways to integrate MTDirectionsKit into your App:

  • 1. Include the binary framework MTDirectionsKit.framework

    This is the easiest way to integrate MTDirectionsKit into your App and therefore the recommended one. Just drag MTDirectionsKit.framework onto the Frameworks-group of your App, you don't need to setup a User Header Search Path or a Target Dependency.

    Next add the import statement

    #import <MTDirectionsKit/MTDirectionsKit.h>

    everywhere you want to use MTDirectionsKit and you are good to go. See the image for visual hints on how to integrate MTDirectionsKit.framework:

  • 2. Add MTDirectionsKit.xcodeproj as a dependency

    First copy the whole MTDirectionsKit project into your applications root folder. Then locate the "MTDirectionsKit.xcodeproj" file inside your applications root folder and drag it onto the root of your Xcode project's Frameworks-group.

    Add MTDirectionsKit as target dependency of your target under the tab Build Phases and drag libMTDirectionsKit.a onto the section 'Link Binary with Libraries' of your target.

    Next add the import statement

    #import <MTDirectionsKit/MTDirectionsKit.h>

    everywhere you want to use MTDirectionsKit and you are good to go. See the image for visual hints on how to integrate MTDirectionsKit.framework:

Independent of which way to integrate MTDirectionsKit you choose, you have to add

  • CoreLocation.framework
  • MapKit.framework
  • libxml2.dylib

to your target's linked libraries found under Build Phases.

Requirements

MTDirectionsKit needs Xcode ≥ 4.3, iOS Base SDK ≥ 5.1 and Clang LLVM >= 3.1 to compile. It doesn't work with the old LLVM GCC compiler. MTDirectionsKit works on a deployment target of ≥ iOS 4.0 and can be used in ARC or non-ARC applications.
